## Overview

Talisman: Hexed replaces the fixed Talisman board with 61 hexagonal tiles arranged in concentric rings. Every game deals a new map — same characters, same cards, same spells, completely different journey.

The square Talisman board gets boring fast — same path, same encounters, same finale every game. Hexagonal tiles let us deal a new map each session and keep the journey unpredictable.

<div class="highlight">
<div class="box-title">What This Mod Does</div>

**Replaces:** The Talisman board (outer, middle, and inner regions).

**Keeps:** All characters, adventure cards, spell decks, purchase cards, stranger cards, warlock quests, and core rules. Your entire encounter library is untouched.
</div>

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 2</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## The Four Rings

61 tiles arranged in concentric rings:

- **Outer Ring (24 tiles)** — The world. Forests, villages, plains, and ruins. This is where most early-game exploration happens.
- **River Ring (18 tiles)** — Connects across rings. 10 of these are water tiles that require a boat token, raft, or relevant character ability to cross.
- **Middle Ring (12 tiles)** — The highlands. More dangerous encounters, better rewards. Equivalent to the original middle region.
- **Inner Ring (6 tiles)** — The dungeon approach. The final gauntlet before the Crown.
- **Central Tile (1 tile)** — The Crown of Command. Unchanged from base Talisman.

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 3</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## Special Tiles

- **10 water tiles** connect across rings via the river. They act as bridges between rings but require passage ability.
- **4 desert tiles** act as Talisman's traditional "Desert" region — no rest, no healing.
- **Remaining tiles** map onto the original board's encounter spaces, redistributed randomly each game.

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 4</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## Movement

Roll 1d6 and move that many tiles in any direction along legal edges. You may cross river tiles only with a boat token, raft, or relevant character ability.

<div class="box">
<div class="box-title">Key Differences from Base Talisman</div>

- Movement is **multidirectional** — you choose which direction to travel along hex edges, not just clockwise/counter-clockwise.
- **River crossing** requires specific items or abilities. Without them, river tiles block your path.
- The **shortest path** between two points changes every game because tile layout is randomised.
</div>

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 5</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## Setup

1. Separate tiles by ring (outer/river/middle/inner/central).
2. Shuffle each ring's tiles face-down.
3. Deal the central tile first, then build outward: inner ring around it, then middle, then river, then outer.
4. Place tiles face-up as you lay them — the map is open information.
5. Set up characters, decks, and tokens as per standard Talisman 4e rules.

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 6</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## What Hasn't Changed

All Talisman 4e (revised) cards, characters, spell decks, and adventure cards work as-is. Hexed only replaces the board — your encounter library is untouched.

- Character abilities, stats, and progression: unchanged.
- Combat, craft, and strength: unchanged.
- Spells, objects, followers: unchanged.
- The Crown of Command endgame: unchanged.
- Expansion content: fully compatible.

</div>
