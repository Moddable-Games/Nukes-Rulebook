## Overview

Hyper Imperium is a rebalance mod for Twilight Imperium 4th Edition (+ Prophecy of Kings) that accelerates the game without gutting its strategic depth. Five rule changes create a faster, more aggressive, more political experience.

<div class="highlight">
<div class="box-title">The Five Hypers</div>

**Hyper Movement** — Ships move faster. Fleets meet earlier.

**Hyper Trades** — Trade every round, not just when the card is picked.

**Hyper Objectives** — Two public objectives per round. 12 VP to win.

**Hyper Factions** — All 25 factions rebalanced.

**Hyper Agendas** — Politics from round one, not just after Mecatol.
</div>

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 2</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## Hyper Movement

Ships move +1 hex per activation (all ship types).

- This applies to the base movement value of every ship class.
- Gravity Rift, Nebula, and Supernova movement penalties still apply after the bonus.
- Flagship movement bonuses stack with Hyper Movement.

The result: faster fleet positioning and more aggressive early-game posturing. First contact happens 1–2 rounds earlier than base TI4. The galaxy feels smaller and more dangerous.

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 3</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## Hyper Trades

The trade window opens every strategy phase — not just when the Trade strategy card is picked.

- Any player may negotiate trades freely during the strategy phase.
- The Trade strategy card still exists but now grants its holder **2 additional trade goods** on top of normal commodity refresh.
- Promissory notes, ceasefire agreements, and support-for-the-throne trades follow normal rules.

This keeps the economy flowing and makes diplomacy a constant pressure, not a once-per-round event.

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 4</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## Hyper Objectives

Two public objectives are revealed per round instead of one. Victory point target raised to 12 VP (up from 10).

- At the start of each status phase, reveal **2 public objectives** (one Stage I, one Stage II once Stage I deck is exhausted).
- Secret objectives remain unchanged (1 per player, max 3 scored).
- The 12 VP target compensates for faster objective availability — the game doesn't end sooner, but players have more strategic paths to victory.

<div class="box">
<div class="box-title">Why 12 VP?</div>

With double the objectives revealed, 10 VP would shorten the game too aggressively. 12 VP maintains TI4's intended arc (6–8 rounds) while offering more player agency in choosing which objectives to pursue.
</div>

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 5</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## Hyper Factions

Rebalanced faction abilities across all 25 factions. Weaker factions receive buffs; dominant factions are toned down.

### Buffed Factions
- **Arborec** — Letani Warriors now produce on any friendly planet (not just home system).
- **Winnu** — Start with 1 additional commodity. Flagship costs 6 resources (down from 8).
- **Yin Brotherhood** — Devotion triggers on a 9+ (down from 10). Indoctrination range increased to adjacent systems.

### Nerfed Factions
- **Clan of Saar** — Scavenge only triggers once per activation (not per planet).
- **Universities of Jol-Nar** — Research penalty increased to -2 on combat rolls (up from -1).
- **Nekro Virus** — Galactic Threat does not apply to the first technology copied each round.

*Full faction balance document included in the printable reference PDF.*

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 6</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## Hyper Agendas

The agenda phase triggers every round — not just after Mecatol Rex is taken.

- Two agendas are voted on per agenda phase (same as base TI4 post-Mecatol).
- Mecatol Rex no longer gates the agenda phase. Politics are relevant from round one.
- The Speaker still determines vote order. Riders and abilities function normally.
- Controlling Mecatol Rex still grants 1 VP per status phase.

This makes the political game a constant thread throughout the session rather than a late-game addition.
