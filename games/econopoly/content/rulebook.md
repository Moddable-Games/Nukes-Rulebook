## Overview

Econopoly is a rebalance mod for Monopoly that introduces dynamic property pricing, progressive taxation, anti-monopoly regulation, bankruptcy reform, and a hard game timer. The result: a tighter, fairer game that ends in 60 minutes instead of 4 hours.

<div class="highlight">
<div class="box-title">Design Goals</div>

**1. Fix the runaway leader problem.** Progressive tax bands and anti-monopoly clauses keep the richest player in check.

**2. Eliminate player knockout misery.** Bankruptcy reform gives players 3 turns to recover before elimination.

**3. End the game.** A hard timer ensures no session exceeds 90 minutes. No more family arguments.
</div>

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 2</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## Dynamic Pricing

Property prices fluctuate based on how many times they've been landed on. Popular properties become expensive; neglected ones drop in value.

- **At the start of each round**, update property prices on the tracker sheet.
- Each landing on a property increases its purchase price by $50.
- Properties that go 2 full rounds without a landing decrease by $50 (minimum: original printed price).
- Rent is unaffected — only purchase/auction prices change.

<div class="box">
<div class="box-title">Why This Works</div>

Dynamic pricing creates genuine market tension. Landing on a hot property costs more — but waiting means someone else buys it first. Early-game property rushes are no longer automatic.
</div>

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 3</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## Tax Bands

Income tax scales with net worth. The richest player pays the highest rate each time they pass Go.

| Net Worth | Tax Rate | Go Income After Tax |
|-----------|----------|---------------------|
| Under $1,000 | 10% | $180 |
| $1,000–$3,000 | 20% | $160 |
| Over $3,000 | 35% | $130 |

- Calculate net worth = cash + property values (current dynamic price) + house/hotel values.
- Tax is applied when passing Go, reducing the $200 payout.
- If you land on Income Tax, pay the flat $200 OR your band percentage of net worth — whichever is lower.

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 4</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## Anti-Monopoly Clause

Owning all properties of one colour triggers a government intervention.

- When a player completes a colour set, one property from that set is **forcibly auctioned** to the other players on the next turn.
- The monopoly holder chooses which property to auction.
- Auction starts at 50% of the property's current dynamic price.
- The monopoly holder may not bid.

<div class="meltdown-box">
<div class="box-title">Important</div>

Monopolies are powerful but temporary. You can rebuild them — buying back the auctioned property is legal — but each time you complete the set, another auction triggers. Houses/hotels remain on the properties through auction.
</div>

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 5</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## Bankruptcy Reform

Bankrupt players enter "debt mode" for 3 turns before elimination.

- During debt mode, you can still: trade, sell properties, mortgage, negotiate deals.
- You cannot: buy new properties, build houses, or collect rent.
- If you reach $0+ cash by the end of 3 debt-mode turns, you exit debt mode and resume normal play.
- If you cannot, you are eliminated.

This prevents early-game knockouts from ruining the experience for any player.

</div>

<div class="section"><div class="eyebrow"><span class="eyebrow-badge">Section 6</span><span class="eyebrow-line"></span></div>

## Game Timer

The game ends after **90 minutes** or when **2 players are eliminated**, whichever comes first.

- When the timer ends, complete the current round (so all players have equal turns).
- The player with the highest net worth wins.
- In case of a tie, the player with the most cash (not including property) wins.

No more 4-hour Monopoly marathons.

</div>
